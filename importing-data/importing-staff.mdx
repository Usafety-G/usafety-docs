---
title: "Importing staff via CSV"
description: "Bulk-onboard employees into Usafety from a spreadsheet."
---

The fastest way to populate a new Usafety organisation is a CSV import. Usafety's importer auto-detects common column names (Name, Email, Department, Job Title, Instructor, etc.) and lets you map anything else manually.

## Before you start

Export your HR data to a CSV with at least these columns:

- **Name** (full name)
- **Email** (work email address)

Optional but recommended:

- **Department**
- **Job title**
- **Manager email**
- Any custom fields you use in Usafety

<Tip>
  The importer handles messy headers — "Employee Name", "Full Name", "emp_name" will all auto-map to the Name field. If a column doesn't auto-detect, you'll map it manually in the next step.
</Tip>

## Run the import

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Staff → Import / Export → Import CSV">
    Click **Staff** in the sidebar. In the top-right, open the **Import / Export** menu and choose **Import CSV**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Upload the CSV">
    Drag and drop or select your file. Usafety parses the first row as headers and shows a preview.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the mapping">
    The importer suggests a target field for each column. Check each one — especially for custom fields — and adjust if needed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start the import">
    Click **Import**. The job runs in the background; you can close the tab and come back.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the results">
    When the job finishes, you'll see a summary: how many rows imported, how many skipped, and why. Any failed rows are downloadable as a CSV you can fix and re-import.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What happens on duplicates

Usafety matches on email address. If an employee already exists:

- **Existing fields** are left alone (so you don't overwrite data people have updated)
- **Empty fields** are filled in from the CSV
- **Custom fields** merge — existing values win

If you want a clean overwrite, delete the employee first and re-import.
