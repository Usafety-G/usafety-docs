## What these regulations cover

The main source of DSE (Display Screen Equipment) law in Ireland is **Chapter 5 of Part 2** of the *Safety, Health and Welfare at Work (General Application) Regulations 2007*, often cited as **S.I. No. 299 of 2007**. The full text is published on the [Irish Statute Book](https://www.irishstatutebook.ie/eli/2007/si/299/made/en/print).

These regulations apply to any employee who **habitually uses a display screen** as a significant part of their normal work — roughly, anyone using a screen for an hour or more at a stretch on most days. Both office-based and remote (home) workers are in scope.

## What employers must do

The regulations impose four main obligations. Usafety's product is designed to help you meet all four.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="1. Analyse each workstation and assess risks" icon="magnifying-glass">
    **The requirement:** Employers must perform an analysis of each workstation to evaluate the safety and health conditions, with particular reference to possible risks to eyesight, physical problems and mental stress.

    **How Usafety helps:** The DSE assessment (remote or in-person) captures chair, desk, monitor, keyboard, posture, lighting and environmental factors. Results feed into the compliance dashboard so you can see who's been assessed, what's outstanding, and who needs equipment.

    See [How assessments work](/dse-assessments/how-assessments-work).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="2. Reduce any risks identified" icon="wrench">
    **The requirement:** Where the workstation analysis shows risks, the employer must take appropriate measures to remedy them.

    **How Usafety helps:** Each assessment generates equipment recommendations (a new chair, a monitor arm, a footrest, etc.) tied to the specific risks found. The fulfilment lifecycle lets you track remediation from *Recommended* through to *Completed*.

    See [Tracking equipment recommendations](/equipment-and-compliance/tracking-recommendations) and [Fulfilment workflow](/equipment-and-compliance/fulfilment-workflow).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="3. Provide information and training" icon="graduation-cap">
    **The requirement:** Employers must ensure DSE users receive training on the use of the workstation before starting work and whenever the organisation of the workstation is substantially modified.

    **How Usafety helps:** The assessment flow walks employees through each risk area, acting as a lightweight training exercise alongside the formal assessment. In-person assessments include a verbal walkthrough by the assessor.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="4. Offer eye and eyesight tests" icon="eye">
    **The requirement:** Employers must provide, on request, appropriate eye and eyesight tests to DSE users — before starting work, at regular intervals, and whenever they have visual difficulties related to DSE work.

    **How Usafety helps:** Usafety doesn't book eye tests directly, but you can add an "Eye test due" custom field to employees (or track it via a custom field on your booking link) to surface who's owed a test at their next assessment.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Reassessment cadence

The regulations don't prescribe a specific reassessment frequency, but both the HSA and industry practice treat **annual reassessment** as the default. Usafety's [compliance dashboard](/equipment-and-compliance/compliance-dashboard) lets you filter employees by *Last Assessment Date > 12 months ago* so you can find who's overdue.

Significant triggers for an off-cycle reassessment include:

- New workstation, new chair, new monitor, or office move
- Shift from office to home-working (or vice versa)
- A new health condition, pregnancy or return-to-work
- An employee complaint or reported discomfort

See [Re-assessments & renewals](/dse-assessments/reassessments).

## Record keeping

The regulations require employers to keep records of assessments and remediation actions for inspection. Usafety stores assessment history, equipment fulfilment status and the comment thread on each record indefinitely, and PDFs can be [exported](/equipment-and-compliance/exporting-equipment-reports) for board or HSA packs.

## If you're audited

If the HSA visits, a typical audit pack includes:

- The list of DSE users in your organisation
- Their most recent completed assessment and date
- Equipment recommendations and fulfilment status
- Evidence that employees were trained
- Evidence that eye tests have been offered

You can assemble most of that from Usafety in a few clicks via the Equipment Compliance dashboard and bulk PDF export. See [Exporting equipment reports](/equipment-and-compliance/exporting-equipment-reports).
