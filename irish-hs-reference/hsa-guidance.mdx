## Who the HSA is

The **Health and Safety Authority (HSA)** is the statutory body responsible for enforcing and providing guidance on workplace health and safety legislation in Ireland. The HSA publishes free guidance documents and checklists alongside the formal regulations, including material specifically on DSE work.

The HSA's DSE homepage is [hsa.ie/eng/Topics/Display_Screen_Equipment_DSE_](https://www.hsa.ie/eng/Topics/Display_Screen_Equipment_DSE_/). It includes a free downloadable checklist, FAQs, case studies, and the enforcement policy.

## What HSA guidance covers

The HSA's published DSE guidance covers:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Who is a DSE user">
    The HSA clarifies that a DSE "user" is any employee who habitually uses DSE as a significant part of their normal work. In practice this means someone who:

    - Uses DSE for an hour or more at a stretch on most days
    - Relies on DSE to do their job (not optional)
    - Requires high attention and concentration

    Both office and home workers are explicitly in scope.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Conducting the assessment">
    The HSA publishes a free **DSE assessment checklist** covering chair, desk, screen, keyboard, mouse, software, environment (lighting, noise, temperature, space) and the interface between user and workstation.

    Usafety's assessment template is designed to cover every item on the HSA checklist, so a completed Usafety assessment satisfies the HSA's recommended scope.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Training">
    HSA guidance recommends that DSE users are trained on:

    - Correct posture and workstation setup
    - How to adjust chair and monitor
    - Importance of breaks and varying tasks
    - How to report discomfort
    - How to request an eye test

    The Usafety assessment flow touches on all of these as the employee completes it.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Eye and eyesight tests">
    HSA guidance confirms that the employer is obliged to provide or pay for:

    - An initial eye test before starting DSE work
    - Further tests at regular intervals or when the user reports DSE-related visual difficulties
    - Corrective appliances (e.g. computer glasses) where the test shows they're specifically needed for DSE work

    Usafety doesn't book or pay for tests directly — you integrate with whatever eye-care provider your organisation already uses. A custom field on the employee record is a simple way to track when someone is due.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Breaks and work organisation">
    HSA guidance recommends frequent short breaks rather than occasional long ones — roughly a 5-10 minute change of task every hour of concentrated DSE work. This is a workplace policy issue rather than a software issue; Usafety doesn't enforce or track breaks.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Home and remote workers">
    HSA guidance is explicit that home-based DSE users are covered by the same requirements as office workers. This has become a major focus post-2020 as hybrid working has become standard.

    Usafety's remote assessment flow (Teams video call) is designed for home-based workers specifically — the assessor can see the workstation live without needing to visit.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## The HSA checklist vs the Usafety assessment

Usafety's default assessment template covers every section of the HSA's published DSE checklist. If your organisation has a specific internal checklist you want to use instead, the Usafety team can customise the assessment template for you — contact [info@usafety.ie](mailto:info@usafety.ie).

## HSA inspections

If the HSA visits your premises, inspectors will typically ask for:

1. Your written DSE risk assessment — Usafety records count
2. Evidence of remediation where risks were found — the fulfilment lifecycle records this
3. Evidence that employees were trained — assessment completion records
4. Evidence that eye tests have been offered and results acted on — your internal tracking

You can assemble most of this from Usafety via the [Equipment Compliance dashboard](/equipment-and-compliance/compliance-dashboard) and [bulk PDF export](/equipment-and-compliance/exporting-equipment-reports).
