This page is for senior leaders who've been sent a Usafety equipment report for sign-off. If you're the H&S manager preparing the report, see [Exporting equipment reports](/equipment-and-compliance/exporting-equipment-reports) instead.

## What you've been sent

Your H&S manager has most likely attached a **PDF** (one employee) or a **ZIP file** (many employees) from Usafety. Each PDF covers one employee and contains:

- **Employee name, department and email**
- **Assessment date** and who carried it out
- **Overall compliance status** — *Compliant*, *Non-compliant* or *Not assessed*
- **Equipment required** — each recommended item with model, supplier, unit cost and quantity
- **Fulfilment status** of each item — where it currently sits in the ordering lifecycle
- **Comments thread** with any context the H&S team has added (supplier calls, PO numbers, escalation notes)

## What you're being asked to decide

For each recommendation, you're approving one of three outcomes:

1. **Approve and order** — the H&S manager can place the order immediately
2. **Approve with changes** — a different supplier, different model, different quantity, or combine with an existing order
3. **Reject** — with a reason (cost, alternative accommodation already provided, item is duplicate, etc.)

Your decision feeds directly into the Usafety **fulfilment lifecycle** — the H&S manager will mark each item *Approved*, *Ordered* or *Rejected* accordingly.

## The legal context

Under the **Safety, Health and Welfare at Work (General Application) Regulations 2007** (S.I. No. 299 of 2007, Chapter 5 of Part 2), Irish employers must ensure each DSE workstation meets defined ergonomic requirements. Where an assessment identifies equipment needs, the employer is obliged to provide what's reasonably practicable.

Rejecting a recommendation is legally allowed, but the reason should be documented and — if the employee has raised a health concern — a written record of the decision should be kept. Usafety stores your comment on each item as part of the audit trail automatically.

## What happens after you approve

Once you've signed off:

1. Your H&S manager updates each item in Usafety to **Approved** or **Rejected**.
2. Approved items move through **Ordered → Delivered → Installed → Completed** as the purchasing process runs.
3. When every item for an employee is **Completed**, that employee's compliance status flips from *Non-compliant* to *Compliant* automatically.

You'll see the impact next time you're sent a report.

## Questions SLT often ask

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Why is the recommended supplier more expensive than our usual one?">
    The equipment catalogue is set by your H&S manager. If you use a specific procurement partner, ask them to update the catalogue so future recommendations default to your supplier's SKU.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can we bulk-approve items instead of doing one at a time?">
    Yes. Your H&S manager can tick rows in the Equipment Compliance table and mark multiple items as *Approved* in one action.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What happens if we decline a recommendation the employee asked for?">
    Usafety keeps the declined item in the audit trail. If an employee has raised a health concern, Irish workplace H&S guidance strongly recommends a written record explaining the decision, which can be added as a comment on the Usafety record.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
