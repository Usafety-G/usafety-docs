Usafety doesn't currently auto-generate a branded monthly report. Instead, your H&S manager typically assembles a pack by hand using the numbers on the dashboard plus an exported batch of equipment reports. This page describes what's in a well-assembled SLT pack so you know what to ask for.

<Note>
  An automated monthly compliance summary is on the Usafety roadmap. Until it ships, the structure below reflects what most organisations put together manually.
</Note>

## The four things a good monthly pack contains

### 1. Headline numbers

A single slide or page with the four numbers worth watching:

- **% of staff assessed in the last 12 months** (target: 100%)
- **% of staff currently compliant** (target: improving trend)
- **Equipment items awaiting SLT approval** (your decision queue)
- **Equipment items stuck in *Ordered*** (supplier chase list)

All four come from the **Reports → Equipment Compliance** page.

### 2. Outstanding decisions for SLT

The batch of PDFs your H&S manager has exported from Usafety, filtered to **Compliance = Non-compliant + Remediation = To Do**. These are the employees whose equipment you're being asked to sign off on.

Each PDF includes the employee's recommendations with cost, so finance can tally the committed spend.

See [Approving equipment purchases](/for-senior-leaders/approving-equipment) for what you're being asked to decide.

### 3. Progress since last month

A short narrative from your H&S manager covering:

- How many new assessments completed
- How many equipment items moved from *Approved* to *Ordered*, or from *Installed* to *Completed*
- Anyone with overdue re-assessments (more than 12 months since last assessment)

### 4. Exceptions and escalations

Anything that's stuck, blocked or getting worse:

- Bookings cancelled and not rebooked
- Employees who've been sent a booking link but haven't booked
- Suppliers missing their committed delivery dates
- Contested recommendations where SLT and the H&S team disagree
