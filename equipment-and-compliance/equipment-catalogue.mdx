---
title: "The equipment catalogue"
description: "Set up your organisation's master list of approved equipment."
---

The equipment catalogue is your organisation's master list of chairs, monitors, monitor arms, footrests, keyboards and anything else a DSE assessment might recommend. When an assessor generates a recommendation, they pick from this catalogue — so getting the catalogue right at the start makes everything downstream cleaner.

## Opening the catalogue

Go to **Settings → Equipment**. You'll land on the *Equipment Management* page with every piece of equipment your organisation has added.

## Adding an item

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click Add equipment">
    The button is in the top-right of the Equipment Management page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in the details">
    Required fields:

    - **Name** — e.g. *Humanscale Freedom Chair*
    - **Category** — what kind of equipment it is (chair, monitor, footrest, etc.)

    Optional but strongly recommended:

    - **Description** — any additional spec or context
    - **Model** — the manufacturer's model identifier
    - **Part number** — the internal SKU or part code you use for ordering
    - **Supplier** — who you buy it from
    - **Cost per unit** — the unit price (unlocks budget roll-up in SLT reports)
    - **Currency** — defaults to EUR
    - **Link URL** — a link to the supplier page, your internal procurement portal, or spec sheet

    Two flags to be aware of:

    - **Is active** — unticking hides the item from the assessor's picker without deleting the history
    - **Is default** — marks the item as your organisation's default choice for its category
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    The item is immediately available for assessors and admins to recommend.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Editing and archiving

Click any row to edit it. There's no separate "archive" action — instead, untick **Is active** on the item and it'll be hidden from the assessor picker while keeping its history in every assessment that referenced it.

## Why the catalogue matters

- **Consistency.** Assessors pick from the same list every time, so "ergonomic chair" means the same thing across every report.
- **Budget visibility.** Cost-per-unit rolls up into SLT reports and the compliance dashboard so finance can see total committed spend at a glance.
- **Faster fulfilment.** Part number, supplier and link URL mean whoever is placing the order has everything they need in one place.
