---
title: "How assessments work"
description: "A 90-second overview of the Usafety DSE assessment lifecycle, from booking link to remediation."
---

A DSE assessment in Usafety flows through six stages, starting with a booking link and ending with your team acting on the findings.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Booking link created">
    An admin creates a booking link in Usafety with the dates, time slots and location where assessments will happen. See [Booking links](/bookings-and-scheduling/booking-links).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Link approved">
    The Usafety team reviews and approves the link. Same-day during Irish business hours.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Employees book their own slots">
    The admin shares the approved public link. Employees click it, pick a date and time, fill in a short form, and get a confirmation email.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assessment happens">
    On the booked date, the assessment runs — remote (video call with an assessor) or in-person (assessor visits the workstation). The assessor records answers, photos and any equipment recommendations.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Results appear in Assessments">
    Once completed, the record shows up on the **Assessments** page with answers, photos, risk level and recommendations.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Admin reviews and remediates">
    A H&S manager opens the completed assessment, reviews the findings, sets a remediation status, and tracks any equipment recommendations through to fulfilment.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Remote vs in-person

| | Remote | In-person |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **How it runs** | Video call between employee and assessor | Assessor visits the workstation |
| **Duration** | 20–30 minutes | 30–45 minutes |
| **Best for** | Remote workers, annual compliance, large rollouts | New workstations, complex accommodations, return-to-work, complaints |
| **Set on booking link** | Booking type = *Remote* | Booking type = *In person* (with a location) |

Most organisations use remote for the bulk of staff and reserve in-person visits for specific cases.

## What if I don't want employees choosing their own slot?

If you'd rather book on behalf of your staff — for example, you want to schedule a whole department on the same morning — use the [CSV import flow](/importing-data/importing-staff) to bulk-book employees yourself. The self-service booking link is the primary flow for most customers, but imports are a fully supported alternative.
