title: "How assessments work"

---

Every DSE assessment in Usafety follows the same four-step lifecycle, whether it's a quick self-assessment or an in-person visit from a certified assessor.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Sent">
    An admin sends an assessment to an employee. The employee gets an email with a secure link — they don't need a Usafety login.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Completed">
    The employee answers questions about their workstation (chair, monitor, keyboard, lighting, posture), optionally uploads photos, and submits. Results appear on the **Assessments** page immediately.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Reviewed">
    A H&S manager opens the completed assessment, reviews the answers and any flagged issues, and sets a remediation status.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Remediated">
    If equipment is needed, recommendations show up in the Equipment Compliance dashboard and move through the fulfilment workflow until the employee is fully set up.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Self-assessment vs in-person

| | Self-assessment | In-person |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Who runs it** | The employee | A certified assessor |
| **How it's booked** | Admin sends by email | Admin creates a booking in the Calendar |
| **How long it takes** | 10–15 minutes | 30–45 minutes |
| **What it covers** | Standard DSE checklist | Full workstation audit with photos and measurements |
| **When to use it** | Annual compliance, remote workers, large rollouts | Complex accommodations, return-to-work, complaints |

Most organisations use self-assessment as the default and reserve in-person visits for specific cases.
