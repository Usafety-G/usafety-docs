---
title: "Remediation workflow"
description: "Track your team's workflow on acting on assessment findings, alongside Usafety's automatic compliance status."
---

Remediation status is how your team tracks what it's actually doing about assessment findings. It runs alongside the automatic compliance status (which Usafety derives from the data) so you can see both *where the employee stands* and *where your team is on acting on it*.

## The four states

| Status | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| **No Action Needed** | The assessment is fine. No follow-up required. |
| **To Do** | You've reviewed the assessment, there's work to do, but you haven't started yet. |
| **In Progress** | Actively working on it — chasing suppliers, awaiting SLT approval, scheduling an install. |
| **Done** | Everything's been ordered, installed, and confirmed with the employee. |

## Setting the status

Remediation status is edited **inline from the Equipment Compliance table**. Go to **Reports → Equipment Compliance**, find the employee row, and click the dropdown in the *Remediation Status* column to change it.

The Quick View side panel shows the current remediation status as a read-only badge — it's set from the table, not from inside the Quick View.

## Why it exists

Usafety's automatic compliance status only knows about the data — it'll flip to *compliant* the moment all recommended equipment is marked *Completed*. But that moment might be weeks away, and in the meantime SLT and your H&S committee want to know *what you're doing about it*.

Remediation status fills that gap. It's your workflow, not Usafety's judgement.

## Filtering by remediation status

On the **Equipment Compliance** dashboard, filter by remediation status to answer specific questions:

- **To Do** — my backlog
- **In Progress** — things I should chase this week
- **Done** — items to spot-check in the next reassessment cycle
- **No Action Needed** — assessments you've already cleared
